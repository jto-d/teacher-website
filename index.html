Insight into Mr. Boogaard

Mr. Boogaard has taught numerous different history courses, some including: World Civilizations, CP and Accel US History I, AP US II, Criminal Law, Economics, AP Government & Politics, and so much more. His classes are lively, contain tons of discussion and investigation. Mr. Boogard is able to bring both past and present issues to life for his students. His love for learning even extends further. Ms. Smart, a colleague of his wrote that, "He must hold a record for attending NEH Seminars, and many summers he will attend multiple workshops. He has been a reaeder for Advanced Placement US History II essays and organized students enrichment trips to China, Australia, France, Malta, and Ireland".

From Evan Lapid (Class of 2022 and Co-Creat0r of this Website): Mr Boogaard is an extremely interesting and inspiring teacher. From the way he conducts his class to the ways he is able to engage his students, every class taken with him is amazing. I have taken two classes with Mr. Boogaard, Accel US I and AP GOV, and in both I have always left feeling like I wanted more. The projects we do in class and the way Mr. Boogaard connects the topics to the real world is extremely fascinating. This past year in AP Gov we have done multiple projects that let me experience the day to day activites of members of Congress, and it was extremely interesting. I have also partaken in both of Mr. Boogaards clubs which I found extremly fun. While I liked Academic Challenge more, I was involved with MUN in other ways and I saw that every student there enjoyed what they were doing. Overall, I Mr. Boogaard was generally a great teacher and I hope everyone has the chance to take one of his classes.

Accelerated US History


(Grades 10, 11) – This course is designed to study the United States from early exploration and settlement to Reconstruction. Current events and geographical studies are an integral part of the course requirements.

AP US Government


(Grade 12) - Advanced Placement U.S. Government and Politics allows students with a high interest level in government, politics, history, or law to gain a deeper understanding of how our federal government evolved and how it works today. Units addressed include the constitutional underpinnings of the U.S. Government, political beliefs and behaviors, political parties, interest groups, the mass media, the Congress, the Presidency, the bureaucracy, the Federal Courts, public policy, civil rights, and civil liberties. In addition to the textbook and supplemental readings, students are expected to follow national news events closely throughout the year. This course prepares students to take the AP test in U.S. Government and Politics in May.

CP Economics


(Grades 10, 11, 12) - This is a semester-long elective course open to juniors and seniors that allows students to gain an introduction to the study of criminal law and criminal justice and the problems posed in the 21st century. Topics to be addressed include the nature and causes of crime in America; crimes against the person; crimes against property; defenses; the criminal justice process – including criminal investigations, pretrial proceedings, trial procedures, sentencing, and corrections; the juvenile justice system; and civil rights protections for both criminals and victims.

CP Criminal Law


(Grades 10, 11, 12) - This is a dynamic examination of the fundamental principles which govern economic activities at both the level of the individual and that of the aggregate economy. In addition to preparing a student in both microeconomics and macroeconomics, a major emphasis of this course is on the development of critical-thinking skills and the application of economic principles and methodologies in problem-solving. Consequently, the course incorporates a wide variety of activities including formal instruction in writing, debates, presentations, research projects, and problem-solving competitions in addition to traditional lectures, incorporation of community resources, exams, and simulation activities
